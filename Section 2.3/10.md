Let (n = 2^k) (since (n) is a power of 2), and (T(1)=1).

---

### 1) (T(n)=T(n/2)+1)

Unroll:
[
T(n)=T(n/2)+1 = T(n/4)+2 = \cdots = T(1)+k
]
since after (k=\log_2 n) halvings you reach 1.

So:
[
T(n)=1+\log_2 n
]

---

### 2) (T(n)=2T(n/2)+1)

Let (n=2^k) and define (S(k)=T(2^k)). Then:
[
S(k)=2S(k-1)+1,\quad S(0)=T(1)=1
]

Solve by unrolling:
[
S(k)=2^k S(0) + \sum_{i=0}^{k-1}2^i
=2^k\cdot 1 + (2^k-1)=2^{k+1}-1
]

Convert back ((2^k=n)):
[
T(n)=2n-1
]

---

### 3) (T(n)=2T(n/2)+n)

Again (S(k)=T(2^k)):
[
S(k)=2S(k-1)+2^k,\quad S(0)=1
]

Unroll:
[
S(k)=2^k S(0) + \sum_{j=1}^{k} 2^{k-j}\cdot 2^j
]
But (2^{k-j}\cdot 2^j = 2^k), so the sum is (k\cdot 2^k).

Thus:
[
S(k)=2^k + k2^k = (k+1)2^k
]
Convert back:
[
T(n)=n(\log_2 n + 1)
]

---

### 4) (T(n)=4T(n/2)+3)

Let (S(k)=T(2^k)):
[
S(k)=4S(k-1)+3,\quad S(0)=1
]

Unroll:
[
S(k)=4^k S(0) + 3\sum_{i=0}^{k-1}4^i
=4^k + 3\cdot \frac{4^k-1}{4-1}
=4^k + (4^k-1)=2\cdot 4^k -1
]

Since (4^k = (2^k)^2 = n^2):
[
T(n)=2n^2 - 1
]

---

## Final answers

* (\boxed{T(n)=1+\log_2 n})
* (\boxed{T(n)=2n-1})
* (\boxed{T(n)=n(\log_2 n+1)})
* (\boxed{T(n)=2n^2-1})
